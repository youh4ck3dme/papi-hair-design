# Week 4: Reporting, Compliance, And Buyer Materials

## Objective
Turn the platform from a technically strong product into something a buyer can understand, trust, and evaluate with real operational evidence.

## Why This Week Exists
- Without reporting, the product still relies too much on narrative.
- Without compliance and offboarding clarity, buyers see risk.
- Without buyer materials, sales conversations keep falling back to technical explanations.

## Scope
1. Reporting and KPI scope
2. Compliance pack scope
3. Buyer-facing material pack
4. Exit criteria for a stronger pilot-to-sale story

## Deliverables
- Reporting spec for:
  - bookings
  - no-shows
  - active staff
  - retention / repeat visits
- Compliance pack outline:
  - DPA
  - export
  - offboarding
  - data termination flow
- Buyer materials pack outline:
  - one-page deck
  - demo video script
  - FAQ / objections sheet
  - asset inventory summary

## Execution Tasks
### Reporting
- Decide which metrics must be visible first for owners.
- Separate internal diagnostics from owner-facing operational reporting.
- Define the minimum trustworthy KPI set before building dashboards.

### Compliance Pack
- Define what a serious buyer expects on paper.
- Clarify export, retention, and offboarding behavior.
- Make sure legal/compliance scope matches current system reality.

### Buyer Materials
- Prepare materials for non-technical review.
- Make the story understandable without a repo walkthrough.
- Keep PAPI-specific brand references separate from white-label sales material.

### Guardrails
- Do not invent metrics we cannot calculate reliably.
- Do not promise compliance artifacts that are not backed by process.
- Do not sell a white-label future using only PAPI-branded proof points.

## Acceptance Criteria
- The first KPI/reporting set is clearly defined.
- Compliance pack scope is concrete and credible.
- Buyer materials are defined as a reusable pack, not as one-off notes.
- A buyer can understand the product without deep technical context.

## Review Checklist
- Are the proposed metrics operationally useful?
- Is the compliance scope realistic for current maturity?
- Would a buyer understand the offer from these materials alone?
- Is the white-label sales layer separated from the PAPI production identity?
