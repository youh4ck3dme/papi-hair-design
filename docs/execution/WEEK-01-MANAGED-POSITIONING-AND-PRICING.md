# Week 1: Managed Positioning And Platform Pricing

## Objective
Shift the product story from "self-serve SaaS" to a managed booking and operations product, and define a buyer-facing pricing layer separate from the public salon service price list.

## Why This Week Exists
- Buyers should understand what they are paying for without reading technical docs.
- PAPI's public experience must stay brand-clean and customer-focused.
- Platform pricing must be separated from end-customer service pricing before any serious pilot conversations.

## Scope
1. Managed product messaging blueprint
2. Platform pricing page blueprint
3. Setup fee + monthly retainer packaging
4. Sales positioning guardrails for SK/CZ market

## Deliverables
- A final messaging matrix:
  - current wording
  - target wording
  - banned wording
- Wireframe/content spec for a software pricing page
- Suggested packaging model:
  - setup fee
  - monthly retainer
  - optional add-ons
- CTA and FAQ copy draft for buyer-facing use

## Execution Tasks
### Messaging
- Rewrite product description as a managed service with guided onboarding.
- Remove any remaining self-serve or overly enterprise wording from buyer-facing materials.
- Define one-sentence positioning for:
  - salon owners
  - agencies / partners
  - future white-label sales

### Pricing Structure
- Define a simple baseline pricing model:
  - `199-490 EUR` setup fee
  - `29-79 EUR / month / location`
- Decide what is included in setup vs monthly support.
- Decide which services stay manual and which become productized later.

### Pricing Page Blueprint
- Separate `software/platform pricing` from `service pricing`.
- Draft structure:
  - Hero
  - What is included
  - Pricing cards
  - Add-ons / setup expectations
  - FAQ
  - CTA
- Keep this page outside the PAPI customer-facing booking narrative.

### Guardrails
- Do not position as fully self-serve yet.
- Do not promise multi-tenant onboarding that does not exist.
- Do not mix end-customer salon pricing with software/platform pricing.

## Acceptance Criteria
- Managed messaging is clearly documented.
- Pricing structure is simple enough to explain in one call.
- A buyer can understand the commercial offer without reading technical docs.
- The work does not reintroduce demo-style noise into the PAPI production app.

## Review Checklist
- Is the wording believable for the current product maturity?
- Is the pricing simple enough for SK/CZ buyers?
- Is there a clear distinction between platform pricing and salon service pricing?
- Does this avoid overselling self-serve SaaS readiness?
